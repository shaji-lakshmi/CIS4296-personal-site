My Laksha

  The Idea:

Creating an eportfolio has been a professional development goal since 2018. 2018 brought on a lot of opportunities for me to develop my skills professionally. I wanted to create a space where I am able to collect and showcase the projects that I take part in throughout my college and professional career.

  The Theme:

The theme for the website evolved throughout time. While the content that I wished to display remained the same, the color palette, the layout, and the navbars took some evolution before they were implemented on the site. The color palette was originally decided to be black, red and white. However, I wanted the content that I put on the site to be effectively highlighted. Giving a darker background to the site, made most of the pictures along with the text stand out on the page. The yellow color is sprinkled across the site because it is muted enough that it mimics white and adds a pop of color to the set template layout.

The sidenav was implemented in order to replace the outdated top navigation bar. I wanted to have the navigation be easily accesible to the viewer. Therefore, I made the navigation options along with my logo and name be displayed directly in line with the content and travel with each scroll on the page. This increases the accessibility that the viewer has to their navigation options throughout the site.

Team Collaborations ‐ 2019


TedxTempleU

  My Role:


I was given the oppurtunity to work alongside a team consisting of a programmer, graphic designer and photographer in order to create and maintain an event landing page for TEDxTempleU.

DISCLAIMER: The site was only paid for and active for one year. Therefore, I have provided video captures of the site as it was built at the time. However, if you were to look up tedxtempleu.com after 2020, the site may not be available for view.

  Purpose:

The original purpose of the site was to be a platform for ticket sales. The page slowly evolved into a landing page for the event being hosted at Temple University. Much of the site contains information about the event such as venue, theme, and speaker bios. Alongside a team, we constructed the overall theme, content layout and functionlities of the page to create the best representation of all available information about the event in April 2019.

  Theme:

The theme was predetermined to by the organizer to be XXL, Explore, Experience, Live. The colors and logo were also provided and resembled the theme colors of the school. The red, black and white were carried out throughtout the entire site to keep a cohesive feel. The standard layout of content on the page was carried out by my programming partner adn the page was further developed to add elements and graphic content based on discussions conducted among the team.

  Home Page Implementations:


Full Homepage:


Navigation:

I had the idea to replace the use of a traditional top navbar from the page. While some of the additional pages utilize a top nav, I proposed the idea of implementing tiles as a method of navigation. Working with the graphic designer, we were able to come up with this "word wall" that acts as a navigation tool. This creates a separation between the content blocks, and provides a new way for users to navigate the site.



Timer:

The timer was implemented in order to have a record of when the event will take place. It is displayed at the center of the page, and the countback adds anticipation for the event day.

  Team Page Implementations:

Our team consisted of approximately 23 people and we wanted to have a space to introduce them in the best way possible. This page's construction required a lot of data collection, and research. I wanted to make the page centered around each team member so, the first step in creating this page consisted of having outreach with each team member using google forms. Then, I wanted to find a space saving way to display all of the information that was provided to me.

Utilizing cards were the best option available. I am able to manipulate their size using basic bootstrap principles. This allows me to compress the amount of space dedicated to each team member. The static information which is their name and their department were displayed using the card body properties. The additional information that we recieved from each team member is hidden until the user hovers on each team member's picture. Again since there are many people on the team, the design of the page needed to utilize the amount space given in the most effective way possible.

  About Page Implementations:

This page's purpose is to be an information page that provides the user with the necessary information about the event's venue, theme, and date. This page layout first introduces the theme which cathes the eye of the user and explains the page title, XXL. Then poster on the left hand side ties the theme together and the information is condensed and classified to one place. Clear concise titles guide the user to the information that they are looking for which often consists of what, when and where. The counter on the bottom keeps the user informed of how many days they have before the event. In a way the counter added a level of anticipation for the event so, it was translated from the home page to the about page. The counter that is shown in the video below does not include the counter that was used in the original release of the page. This video was taken when I was creating my portfolio which was months after the event took place.

  Gallery Page Implementations:

This page holds some of the photographs that were taken during the planning stages of the event. This page required me to use both bootstrap and use custom css to create the overlapping effect that is carried out throughout the page.

Temple University Alert

  The Project:

Pull in information from the Temple University weather alert system to be displayed on noncredit.temple.edu. The weather alert will be prompted everytime there is an alert available on the server. Otherwise, the alert will remain hidden and the pages across the site will operate and appear as normal.

  The Design:

When there is a weather emergency, there will be a yellow box that will pop up on above all noncredit online content. This box will initially display a summary message for the user followed by a read more button. All information that you would need is available within the given yellow area. The information is pulled in automatically as Temple releases weather alerts. The alerts will push existing pages down and will be displayed across all program office pages along with the homepage.

Continuing Education Systems ‐ 2019


Continuing Education Department Homepage Redesign

Original Home Page:
  The Original Homepage:

As highlighted above, the original home page was just a collection links and paragraphs organized into three columns. This set up was rarely updated and the content didn't really serve a purpose and most importantly, it did not provide the users with any information about the department or the services that it had to offer.

  The Project:

Working in a team of five people including our supervisor, redesign the department homepage to answer the following questions for the user:

- What is Continuing Education?
- What are the benefits of me taking continuing education courses?
- What services are offered?
As a team, we were able to choose and implement key features to the homepage that would effectively communicate the answers to the proposed questions in the most transparent way possible. Additionally, the redesign project improved the navbar to imporve site wide navigation.

  What is Continuing Education?

In order to answer this question, I took lead in creating a video that would highlight some of the core programs and services that continuing education offers. The video utilizes mainly Temple provided visuals in their database. The interface that was chosen to best display all desired content was an HTML carousel. The video became part of the top carousel that included additional information about registration timelines, program oriented courses and services as marketing ads.
